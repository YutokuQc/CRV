ACC · Card Relative Value
How cards are evaluated and scored
CRV METHODOLOGY · v0.1
01 · Foundation
The Three Pillars
1
Pillar One
Base Score
Every card starts at 1.0 — representing 100% of its stat value. This is the neutral baseline before any ability is considered.
BASE = 1.0 (always)
2
Pillar Two
Stat Split
A card's stats are not equal. HP is worth double Damage because it takes 2 hits to kill a card of equal stats. This ratio drives every calculation.
DMG = 33.33% · HP = 66.66%
3
Pillar Three
Ability Value
Ability score is added on top of the base. Calculated from proc rates, effect strength, and number of triggers within the 4-turn test environment.
FINAL = 1.0 + ABILITY%
02 · Stat Weight
Why HP Outweighs Damage
Stat Distribution Bar
DMG 33.33%
HP 66.66%
Damage — 33.33 points
HP — 66.66 points
A card with 100 DMG and 200 HP requires exactly 2 basic attacks to defeat a card of equal stats. This means HP is inherently worth twice as much — losing it costs you twice as many turns.

When a card buffs its own HP by 20%, the value gained is 66.66 × 0.20 = 13.33 points. The same 20% to DMG only yields 33.33 × 0.20 = 6.66 points. Same percentage, different impact.
03 · Test Environment
Lab Conditions
4v4 Format
All evaluations use a standardized 4v4 match — the most common play format and the format used in PvP. Both teams have 4 cards of identical stats to the card being evaluated.
4 Turns · 8 Interactions
A full evaluation runs 4 turns. Each turn = 2 interactions between active cards. Basic attacks are present but deal no damage — they're universal and not a differentiating factor.
Min / Average / Max
Every ability is scored three ways. Min = worst realistic outcome. Average = statistically expected procs. Max = perfect RNG, all conditions met, absolute ceiling — still within 4 turns.
Ceiling Values
All values represent idealistic outcomes. Where specific or unlikely conditions are required, they are flagged in the Author's Note. The goal is to expose maximum potential, not average performance.
